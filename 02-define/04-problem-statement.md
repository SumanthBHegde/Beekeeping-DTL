## What a problem statement is for this project

The problem statement is the project’s point of view for Ideation and Prototyping. It translates the research into a focused user-centred challenge, without jumping to a solution and without becoming so broad that it loses direction.

For this project, it should stay narrow enough to be realistic for a course assignment, but still broad enough to allow multiple solution directions such as tools, workflow changes, or training support.

## Criteria used

- Focused on the primary user group: small-scale farmers and novice beekeepers.
- Grounded in empathy data, pain points, affinity themes, and need statements.
- Avoids solution language such as "build an app" or "create a device."
- Narrow enough to support ideation and prototyping within the scope of this project.

## Draft versions

### Draft 1: Too broad

"We need to improve beekeeping for everyone."

Why it was not ideal:

- It includes too many user groups and does not point to a clear design focus.
- It is too vague to guide ideation.

### Draft 2: Too solution-driven

"We need to build a smart hive box for farmers."

Why it was not ideal:

- It jumps straight to a solution instead of describing the actual user need.
- It narrows the project too early and leaves little room for alternatives.

### Draft 3: Better but still too wide

"Small-scale farmers need help managing hives safely and profitably."

Why it was not ideal:

- It is user-centred, but still combines too many different problems in one line.
- It does not clearly highlight the strongest recurring pain: safe, low-disturbance hive care.

## Final problem statement

> **Small-scale farmers and novice beekeepers need a safer and simpler way to inspect and maintain hives because current traditional methods feel risky, time-consuming, and require expertise they do not yet have.**

## Why this statement

This focus came through most strongly across the interviews and empathy artifacts. Fear of stings, lack of training, and repeated hive disturbance appeared again and again, while cost and equipment constraints made high-tech options unrealistic for the primary users. This statement is also useful for the course scope because it leaves room for several directions in ideation, including low-cost tools, improved maintenance workflows, or training-oriented support.
