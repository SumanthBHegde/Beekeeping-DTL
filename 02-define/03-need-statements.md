## Need Statements

Need statements come from the pain points and insights, but they describe what users require rather than proposing a solution. They help translate raw observations into clear design requirements for the next stage.

For this project we use the pattern: **[User group] needs [what] because [surprising insight / reason].**

## For Small-Scale Farmers

- Small-scale farmers need a simple, low-risk way to inspect hives because they fear stings and cannot afford to disturb the colony every time they check it.
- Small-scale farmers need low-cost equipment that fits their existing boxes because expensive imported systems are not viable for their yearly honey output.
- Small-scale farmers need maintenance methods that reduce repeated hive opening because opening the box during heat or monsoon conditions hurts colony health.

Evidence:

- Interview notes in [01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md](../01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md) repeatedly mention sting fear, heat stress, and cost limits.
- The empathy map in [01-empathy/04-empathy-map/README.md](../01-empathy/04-empathy-map/README.md) shows the "Anxious/Fearful" and "Frustrated" feelings most clearly in this group.
- The persona in [01-empathy/05-personas/README.md](../01-empathy/05-personas/README.md) identifies low-cost, modular innovation as a key requirement.

## For Enthusiastic Beginners / Hobbyists

- Enthusiastic beginners need clear, step-by-step guidance on hive management because they lack formal training and often learn only through trial and error.
- Enthusiastic beginners need a visible way to understand hive health because they do not know what is happening inside the box until problems become serious.
- Enthusiastic beginners need reassurance and safer handling support because fear of bees and handling mistakes prevents them from gaining confidence.

Evidence:

- The pain-point list in [01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md](../01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md) includes weak disease detection, wax moth damage, and sting anxiety.
- The affinity map in [02-define/01-affinity-mapping/README.md](01-affinity-mapping/README.md) groups these issues under "Knowledge and training gap" and "Perceived risk and fear."
- The persona file in [01-empathy/05-personas/README.md](../01-empathy/05-personas/README.md) describes beginners as dependent on peer advice, YouTube, and trial-and-error.

## For Society Leaders / FPO Managers

- Society leaders need scalable support methods because many members face the same recurring issues with pests, climate stress, and weak colony management.
- Society leaders need affordable value-addition tools because the biggest business opportunity is not only honey, but also by-products such as royal jelly, pollen, and bee venom.
- Society leaders need solutions that respect bee biology because advanced-looking products fail if they disrupt darkness, bee space, or colony temperature.

Evidence:

- The empathy artifacts in [01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md](../01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md) and [01-empathy/08-insights/README.md](../01-empathy/08-insights/README.md) highlight by-product potential, modularity, and bee-space precision.
- The persona in [01-empathy/05-personas/README.md](../01-empathy/05-personas/README.md) explicitly calls out value-addition and scalable manufacturing.
- The pain-point analysis in [02-define/02-pain-point-analysis.md](02-pain-point-analysis.md) shows that market and trust issues sit alongside operational and training problems.

## For Conscious Consumers

- Conscious consumers need trustworthy, traceable honey because they want proof that the product is pure and not adulterated.
- Conscious consumers need direct access to producers because they want to support farmers rather than pay middlemen.
- Conscious consumers need clearer information about bee products because they are willing to pay more when they understand the value of honey, pollen, and royal jelly.

Evidence:

- The consumer section in [01-empathy/05-personas/README.md](../01-empathy/05-personas/README.md) defines this group as willing to pay a premium for verified quality.
- The market and trust themes in [01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md](../01-empathy/06-pain-points/README.md) point to purity concerns and the retail vs. bulk price gap.
- The empathy map in [01-empathy/04-empathy-map/README.md](../01-empathy/04-empathy-map/README.md) captures the desire for direct sourcing and skepticism about mass-market honey.

## Cross-Group Need Statements

- Users need less disruptive hive interaction because repeated opening harms colony temperature, behavior, and productivity.
- Users need affordable, modular tools because cost is a major adoption barrier across farmers, beginners, and societies.
- Users need clearer visibility into hive health and product quality because confidence grows when people can understand what is happening without guessing.

Evidence:

- These themes appear across the affinity map clusters, the raw pain points, and the empathy map quadrants.
- The strongest repeated ideas are fear, training, cost, and trust.
