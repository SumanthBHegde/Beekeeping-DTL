## How Might We (HMW) Questions

How Might We (HMW) questions are used to reframe our defined problems and insights into actionable design opportunities. Rather than dwelling on the pain points, these questions shift our perspective to look for creative solutions as we transition into the Ideation phase.

## Guidelines followed

- Focused on the user and their specific needs, without pre-defining a solution (e.g., no "how might we build an app...").
- Narrow enough to give us a clear direction, but broad enough to allow for a wide variety of ideas.
- Directly traceable to our empathy insights, affinity map themes, and need statements.

## HMW Questions Grouped by Theme

### Perceived risk and fear

- How might we make hive inspection feel completely safe for beginners who fear bee stings?
- How might we allow farmers to monitor internal hive health without opening the box and triggering aggressive swarming behavior?
- How might we reduce direct contact with bees during routine checks?

### Knowledge and training gap

- How might we help novice farmers accurately diagnose diseases or pest infestations before it's too late, without requiring formal training?
- How might we provide intuitive, on-the-spot guidance to ensure the critical 7mm "bee space" is maintained?

### Time and workload

- How might we reduce the number of times a hive must be physically opened for feeding during the monsoon and summer?
- How might we streamline routine maintenance so it does not add significant labor to the farmer's already busy schedule?

### Cost and equipment

- How might we retrofit existing traditional wooden boxes with safer, modern features so farmers do not have to discard their current investment?
- How might we design solutions that are economically viable for a farmer producing only 10kg of honey a year?

### Market, trust, and value-addition

- How might we help small beekeepers communicate the purity and local origin of their honey so conscious consumers trust them enough to pay a premium?
- How might we empower rural farmers to easily collect and sell highly profitable by-products like Royal Jelly, Pollen, and Bee Venom with minimal extra equipment?

## Next Steps: Ideation

These HMW questions will serve as the direct launchpad for our next phase in the design thinking process. They will be used as the primary starting prompts in our brainstorming sessions located in [03-ideate/brainstorming/](../03-ideate/brainstorming/README.md) to generate a wide range of potential solutions.
