# Empathy — Overview

Project: Efficient Beekeeping and Hive Box

Team: Pavan Mohan Naik, Sumanth Bhaskar Hegde, Pramath Prakash Hegde

Guide: Dr. Mohan Aradhya

## Purpose and Methodology

In the empathy phase of this project, our primary goal was to deeply understand the experiences, pain points, and operational needs of beekeepers facing the realities of Indian apiculture. We employed a multifaceted research approach, combining extensive secondary research into honeybee biology, environmental challenges, and market economics with active field observations. To gather quantitative and structured qualitative data, we designed a targeted questionnaire aimed at distinct survey groups: complete novices, hobbyists, small-scale local operators, large-scale commercial beekeepers, and agricultural society administrators. This allowed us to map the technical, financial, and emotional barriers present in traditional beekeeping.

To capture nuanced, firsthand insights, we conducted in-depth interviews with six key individuals across the beekeeping spectrum. Our subjects included young local beekeepers like Ullas Naik; passionate hobbyists and beginners experiencing the steep learning curve like Akshay Kedla and Sushanth Hegde; researchers and commercial-scale operators like Dhruv Bhat (managing 300 boxes) and Manjunath Hegde; and FPO leaders like Harsha Hegde, who represents the broader market struggles of over 650 registered farmers. Speaking directly with these diverse users provided crucial feedback regarding the unviability of expensive foreign tech (like Flow Hives), the biological realities of "bee space," and the severe impact of heavy maintenance on beginners.

Within this folder, you will find the complete raw and synthesized artifacts of our empathy work. This includes detailed secondary research notes, survey design rationales, and dedicated interview directories containing user profiles, original Kannada transcriptions, and concise English summaries for each of our subjects. These records collectively feed into our subsequent empathy maps, personas, pain points, and insights, forming the foundational evidence that guides our problem definition and helps us ideate a more efficient, user-friendly, and culturally viable bee box.
