**ಜೇನು ಕೃಷಿಕ:** ಮೇನ್ ಪ್ರಾಬ್ಲಮ್ ಅಂದ್ರೆ ವ್ಯಾಕ್ಸ್ ಮಾತ್, ಕಡ್ಜೇನು ಮತ್ತು ಬಿ-ಈಟರ್ಸ್ (Bee-eaters / ಹಕ್ಕಿಗಳು). ಕಡ್ಜೇನಿಗೆ ನಾವೇನು ಮಾಡ್ತೀವಿ ಅಂದ್ರೆ, ಒಂದೆರಡು ಕಡ್ಜೇನನ್ನ ಹೊಡೆದು, ಹಗ್ಗ ಕಟ್ಟಿ ಪೆಟ್ಟಿಗೆ ಹತ್ತಿರ ತೂಗು ಹಾಕ್ತೀವಿ. ಅವಾಗ ಅದಕ್ಕೆ 'ಇದು ಡೇಂಜರ್ ಏರಿಯಾ' ಅಂತ ಗೊತ್ತಾಗಿ, ಬರೋದು ಕಡಿಮೆ ಆಗುತ್ತೆ. ವ್ಯಾಕ್ಸ್ ಮಾತ್‌ಗೆ ಪ್ರಾಪರ್ ಕ್ಲೀನಿಂಗ್ ಮಾಡ್ಲೇಬೇಕು.

**ಜೇನು ಕೃಷಿಕ:** 1500-2000 ರೂ. ಗೆ ಕೊಟ್ರೆ ಒಳ್ಳೇದು. ಈಗಿರೋ ಬಾಕ್ಸ್‌ಗಳು 2500 ರೂ. ತನಕ ಇವೆ. ನೀವು ಮಾಡೋ ಹೊಸ ಮಾಡೆಲ್ ಮಾಡ್ಯುಲರ್ (Modular) ಆಗಿದ್ರೆ, ಎಕ್ಸಿಸ್ಟಿಂಗ್ ಸೂಪರ್ ಚೇಂಬರ್‌ಗೆ (Existing Super) ಫಿಟ್ ಆಗೋ ತರ ಇದ್ರೆ ಕಮರ್ಷಿಯಲ್ ಕೃಷಿಕರು ತಗೊಳ್ತಾರೆ.

**ಜೇನು ಕೃಷಿಕ:** ಹೌದು, ಟ್ರಾನ್ಸ್‌ಪರೆಂಟ್ ವಿಂಡೋ (ಕ್ಲೋಸ್ ಮಾಡೋ ತರ) ಇದ್ರೆ, ಓಪನ್ ಮಾಡದೇನೆ ಮೈಲ್ಡ್ ಇನ್ಸ್ಪೆಕ್ಷನ್ ಮಾಡೋಕೆ ಹೆಲ್ಪ್ ಆಗುತ್ತೆ. ಇನ್ನೊಂದು ಮೇನ್ ಪಾಯಿಂಟ್ ಅಂದ್ರೆ- ಮಳೆಗಾಲದಲ್ಲಿ ಟೆಂಪರೇಚರ್ ಮತ್ತು ಹ್ಯುಮಿಡಿಟಿ ಮೇಂಟೇನ್ ಮಾಡೋದು ಕಷ್ಟ. ಬಾಕ್ಸ್ ತೆಗೆದ್ರೆ ಟೆಂಪರೇಚರ್ ಫ್ಲಕ್ಚುವೇಟ್ ಆಗುತ್ತೆ. ಅದಕ್ಕೆ ಏನಾದ್ರೂ ಸಲ್ಯೂಷನ್, ಹಾಗೂ ಶುಗರ್ ಸಿರಪ್ ಕೊಡೋಕೆ, ಬಾಕ್ಸ್ ಓಪನ್ ಮಾಡದೇನೆ ಹೊರಗಡೆಯಿಂದ (External Feeder) ಫೀಡ್ ಮಾಡೋ ವ್ಯವಸ್ಥೆ ಇದ್ರೆ ಬಹಳ ಹೆಲ್ಪ್ ಆಗುತ್ತೆ. ಈಗಾಗ್ಲೇ ಹುಬ್ಬಳ್ಳಿಯ ಇಂಜಿನಿಯರಿಂಗ್ ಕಾಲೇಜ್‌ನವರು ಫ್ಲೋ-ಹೈವ್ ಥರ ಒಂದು ಪ್ರೋಟೋಟೈಪ್ ಮಾಡಿದ್ರು, ಆದ್ರೆ ಕಾಸ್ಟ್ ಜಾಸ್ತಿ ಆಯ್ತು.

### **Part 2: Concise English Summary**

**Context:** The student interviews Dhruva, an experienced, commercial beekeeper managing ~300 bee boxes at "Kadumane Honey Park" (Dandeli, Karnataka), which has won national awards for eco-tourism. The goal is to gather commercial insights to improve bee-box design.

**Current Practices & Challenges:**

- **Yield & Setup:** He uses traditional 8-frame boxes, harvesting an average of 3 kg (up to 15 kg in peak times) per box per season.
- **Pest Management:** To deter predatory wasps ("Kardjen"), he uses a traditional method: hanging a dead wasp near the hive as a warning signal. Wax moths require regular manual cleaning.
- **Climate Issues:** Monsoon moisture and temperature fluctuations drop the colony's health significantly.

**Feedback on Innovations (Commercial Perspective):**

1. **Flow Hives (Automated Harvesting):** Dhruva dismisses Flow Hives for commercial use. While good for hobbyists with 3-4 boxes, they are too slow for large-scale operations. He prefers traditional frames that can be batch-processed in a centrifuge machine quickly. They are also too expensive.
2. **Pricing & Modularity:** A new bee box should cost between ₹1,500 - ₹2,500. For commercial viability, new designs should be **modular**—meaning new 'super' chambers should fit onto the farmers' existing brood boxes, rather than forcing them to buy entirely new systems.
3. **External Feeders:** Opening the box during winter/monsoons drops the internal temperature. A system to feed sugar syrup from the outside without opening the lid is highly desired.
4. **Transparent Inspection Window:** Validated as a good idea for quick checks, provided it has an opaque flap to keep it dark.
5. **Material Concerns:** While plastic frames are entering the market, he is currently researching the negative impacts of micro-plastics on honey quality and prefers natural materials.

### **Part 3: POV Empathy Phase Survey (Design Thinking)**

**1. User Persona**

- **Profile:** Commercial/Large-Scale Eco-Beekeeper (~300 boxes).
- **Environment:** Dense forest/eco-tourism area (Dandeli - heavy monsoons, wildlife interactions).
- **Motivation:** Efficiency in mass harvesting, maintaining hive health during monsoons, and keeping operations eco-friendly and cost-effective.

**2. Observe (What does the user do?)**

- Harvests honey in bulk using a mechanical centrifuge rather than box-by-box extraction.
- Relies on traditional pest deterrents (hanging dead wasps).
- Struggles to feed bees sugar syrup during the monsoon without exposing the hive to cold air.
- Conducts academic/practical research on the adverse effects of plastics in beekeeping.

**3. Engage (What does the user say/feel? - Quotes & Insights)**

- _"Flow Hives are only for hobbyists. It takes too much time. For 300 boxes, we need to batch process frames in a centrifuge."_
- _"If you design something new, make it modular. It should fit on top of our existing boxes so we don't have to throw them away."_
- _"Opening the box in the rainy season ruins the hive's temperature for two days. We need a way to feed them from the outside."_
- _"Plastic frames are coming into the market, but we are worried about micro-plastics entering the honey."_

**4. Empathize (Pain Points & Frustrations)**

- **Operational Bottlenecks:** Feeding bees in bad weather disrupts hive homeostasis.
- **Scalability:** Innovations like Flow Hives are too slow and expensive for commercial scale.
- **Environmental/Health:** The introduction of plastics into beekeeping might compromise the organic nature of the honey.
- **Cost constraints:** Cannot afford complete system overhauls; solutions must be retrofittable/modular.
