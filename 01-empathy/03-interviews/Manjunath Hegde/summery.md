
### **Part 2: Concise English Summary**

**Context:** Pramod interviews a hobbyist beekeeper with 15-16 years of experience, managing 9 bee boxes primarily for agricultural pollination and personal use. 

**Core Challenges:**
* **Weather & Starvation:** Heavy monsoons prevent bees from foraging, requiring the beekeeper to feed them sugar syrup. 
* **Pesticides:** Chemical sprays on nearby farms are highly toxic to foraging bees and have led to the complete loss of colonies.
* **Predators:** The hives face constant threats from predatory wasps (*Kardjen*), red ants, chameleons, and lizards that eat the bees.
* **Hive Maintenance:** Stands must be kept in water/oil to prevent ants. Inspections are kept minimal (1-3 times a week) to avoid stressing the colony.

**Extraction & Economics:**
* **Extraction:** He uses a manual centrifuge drum. Crucially, he returns the empty wax comb to the hive because bees expend a massive amount of energy to create wax. Reusing the comb drastically increases the speed of the next honey harvest.
* **Cost:** A traditional 6-to-8-frame wooden box costs around ₹1,200 to ₹1,400.
* **Hive Relocation:** While moving hives is fine, he advises keeping them in one spot for at least 3 months so the bees can learn the local foraging routes.

**Feedback on Innovations:**
* Currently does not use any modern mobile apps but is open to trying them.
* Is aware of foreign automatic harvesting boxes (like Flow Hives) and suggests that if a similar, low-cost version could be designed for India, it would be highly beneficial.

### **Part 3: POV Empathy Phase Survey (Design Thinking)**

**1. User Persona**
* **Profile:** The Experienced Hobbyist/Pollination Farmer.
* **Experience:** 15+ years (Managing 9 boxes).
* **Motivation:** Keeping bees primarily to boost crop yield through pollination, with honey as a secondary benefit.

**2. Observe (What does the user do?)**
* Strategically places hives to maximize farm pollination.
* Employs physical barriers (water/oil) to stop ants and reptiles.
* Reuses wax combs during centrifuge extraction to save the bees' energy and speed up production.
* Keeps hives stationary for months at a time to let bees map the local flora.

**3. Engage (What does the user say/feel? - Quotes & Insights)**
* *"I keep them mostly for pollination. Honey is just a bonus for us."*
* *"When neighbors spray pesticides, our bees bring the poison back. It can kill the whole box."*
* *"We put the wax comb back after extracting the honey. Making wax takes too much of their energy. If we give it back, they fill it with honey much faster."*
* *"I've heard of foreign automatic boxes. If you can make something like that at a low price for us, it would be great."*

**4. Empathize (Pain Points & Frustrations)**
* **Chemical Vulnerability:** Feels helpless against the farming practices of neighbors (pesticides) that directly harm his eco-friendly setup.
* **Biological Predators:** Constant battle with not just insects, but larger predators like chameleons and lizards.
* **Energy Economics of Bees:** Understands that wax production is biologically expensive for bees, highlighting why current automatic systems (which often require bees to rebuild wax) might be inefficient if not designed correctly.
* **Lack of Local Tech:** Aware of modern solutions but frustrated by their lack of availability and affordability in the Indian market.
