# English Summary

## Context

An engineering/design student is conducting a user interview with a local beekeeper for a college project based on "Design Thinking." The goal is to understand the current beekeeping process, identify pain points, and gather feedback on potential innovations for bee boxes.

## Current Practices & Yield

The beekeeper manages around 10 boxes, a mix of domestic and wild bees. During the peak season (January to March/April), he harvests honey once a month, yielding about 3 to 3.5 kg per box per harvest (totaling 9-11 kg per year per box). During the off-season (monsoons/when flowers are scarce), the bees must either be relocated to places with flora or fed with sugar syrup; otherwise, they will abandon the hive.

## Major Pain Points

- **Heat Management:** Extreme heat is highly detrimental. If the box gets too hot, bees refuse to move to the upper chamber (super) to store honey. Shading is strictly required.
- **Pest Attacks:** Red ants build nests inside the boxes during the rainy season. More dangerously, "Kardjen" (Wax Moths or predatory wasps) attack the hive, killing bees one by one, which often causes the entire colony to abscond.
- **Hive Inspection:** Opening the box frequently (or using smoke) disturbs the bees, and careless handling can crush bees between the wooden frames.

## Feedback on Innovations

- **Automated/Flow Hives:** The student mentions foreign automated hives that extract honey without opening the box. The beekeeper notes these cost around ₹22,000-₹28,000, which is completely unviable for Indian beekeepers given their annual honey yield. A price point of ₹3,000-₹4,000 would be necessary for local adoption.
- **Transparent Observation Window:** The student suggests adding a glass window to the box. The beekeeper likes the idea, as it would reduce the need to physically open the box to check on the queen's laying status or honey levels. However, he warns that it must have a dark cover to prevent sunlight from creating a greenhouse/lens effect inside the hive.

# Part 3: POV Empathy Phase Survey (Design Thinking)

## 1. User Persona

- **Profile:** Small-scale/Local Beekeeper (managing ~10 boxes).
- **Environment:** Tropical/Sub-tropical region (Karnataka coast/Malnad region - heavy rains, hot summers).
- **Motivation:** Maximum honey yield, keeping the colony healthy, and preventing bees from absconding.

## 2. Observe (What does the user do?)

- Shifts boxes seasonally to find flora.
- Feeds sugar syrup during the rainy/off-season.
- Manually opens boxes 2-3 times a week to inspect the queen, check for pests, and monitor honey levels.
- Uses smoke during extraction, which causes temporary disruption to the colony.
- Manually removes ant nests and fights off predatory insects.

## 3. Engage (What does the user say/feel? - Quotes & Insights)

> "If we don't feed them or shift them during the off-season, they will just fly away."
> "Heat is the biggest problem. If the sun hits the box directly, they won't come up to the 'super' to store honey."
> "Automated boxes are 25k. We only get 10kg of honey a year. The math doesn't work for Indian farmers."
> "A transparent window is a good idea, but only if it has a flap. Sunlight shouldn't enter like a magnifying glass."

## 4. Empathize (Pain Points & Frustrations)

- **Environmental:** Unpredictable weather and intense heat affecting bee productivity.
- **Biological Pests:** Constant threat from red ants and wax moths/wasps.
- **Operational Friction:** Opening the box is a hassle, risks crushing bees between frames, and smoke disturbs the hive's rhythm.
- **Economic Constraints:** Lack of affordable technological upgrades suited for the Indian economic context.
