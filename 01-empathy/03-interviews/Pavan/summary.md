
### **Part 2: Concise English Summary**

**Context:** Pramod, an MCA student, interviews a hobbyist beekeeper from the Malenadu region who has been managing 5-6 bee boxes for the last two years. 

**Core Challenges:**
* **Pests & Predators:** The beekeeper suffered a major loss when a "Wax Moth" infested a box and destroyed the entire colony. Other threats include predatory wasps and small birds.
* **Pesticides:** Chemical sprays from neighboring farms directly kill foraging bees.
* **Weather:** Winters and heavy monsoons cause food scarcity, requiring the beekeeper to artificially feed the bees with sugar syrup once a week. 

**Practices & Harvesting:**
* To protect against ants, he uses oil/water moats at the base of the stands. 
* He reduced hive inspections to once a week because frequent opening stresses the bees.
* He harvests honey using a centrifuge machine. Crucially, he returns the intact wax comb to the hive because bees spend 3 times more energy producing wax than honey. Reusing the comb drastically speeds up the next honey yield.
* Commercial farmers migrate boxes to sunflower fields, but as a hobbyist, he keeps them stationary.

**Tech & Training:** He underwent a 5-day formal training from a recognized Bee Society. He currently doesn't use any modern tech or apps but is highly open to new innovations if they assist in hive monitoring.

### **Part 3: POV Empathy Phase Survey (Design Thinking)**

**1. User Persona**
* **Profile:** The Passionate Hobbyist Beekeeper.
* **Environment:** Malenadu (Western Ghats - heavy rains, rich biodiversity, agricultural neighbors).
* **Motivation:** Beekeeping out of passion and environmental consciousness rather than pure commercial profit.

**2. Observe (What does the user do?)**
* Creates physical barriers (water/oil moats) to protect stands from crawling insects.
* Uses a centrifuge to extract honey safely without destroying the honeycomb structure.
* Feeds bees sugar syrup in a small plate inside the box during winters/monsoons.
* Limits hive inspections to once a week to prevent stressing the colony.

**3. Engage (What does the user say/feel? - Quotes & Insights)**
* *"Wax moths are silent destroyers. If you don't check in time, they eat the wax, larvae, everything. I lost a whole box to it."*
* *"Pesticides from nearby farms are a massive issue. They spray, and our bees die instantly."*
* *"Bees spend 3x more energy making wax than making honey. Returning the empty comb to them increases our next yield much faster."*
* *"I used to open the box out of curiosity, but realized it disturbs them too much."*

**4. Empathize (Pain Points & Frustrations)**
* **Uncontrollable External Factors:** Helpless against pesticides used by neighboring farmers and airborne predators (birds/wasps).
* **Silent Infestations:** Wax moths can destroy a hive from the inside out before a weekly inspection catches them.
* **Inspection Dilemma:** Wants to know if the hive is safe (from moths/starvation) but knows that opening the box causes stress to the bees. Needs a non-intrusive monitoring method.
