# Pain Points

Compiled list of 19 raw pain points derived from our empathy phase interviews and secondary research, tagged by source.

## Safety & Handling
1. Fear of getting stung is the primary barrier preventing new farmers from starting beekeeping. [Harsha Hegde, Sushanth Hegde]
2. Opening boxes to inspect/feed makes the bees aggressive and disturbs the hive. [Ullas Naik, Sushanth Hegde]
3. Heavy traditional wooden boxes cause physical strain for solo beekeepers attempting to shift them. [Akshay Kedla]

## Environmental & Climate 
4. Direct sunlight and extreme heat prevent bees from moving to the super chamber to store honey. [Ullas Naik]
5. Opening the hive in the rainy season rapidly drops internal temperature, causing the colony to suffer for up to two days. [Dhruv Bhat, Akshay Kedla]
6. Colony absconding (flying away) is common when adverse weather hits, leading to high dropout rates. [Harsha Hegde]

## Pests & Diseases
7. Predatory wasps ("Kardjen") attack hives and kill bees one by one. [Ullas Naik, Dhruv Bhat]
8. Wax Moths infest and destroy the comb, often going unnoticed by beginners until it's too late. [Akshay Kedla, Sushanth Hegde]
9. Red ants build nests inside the boxes during the rainy season. [Ullas Naik]
10. Inability to identify diseases early causes beginners to lose entire colonies. [Akshay Kedla]

## Operational & Equipment Flaws
11. Poorly designed boxes fail to maintain the exact 7mm "bee space", causing bees to build messy burr comb that glues frames together. [Akshay Kedla]
12. Frame extraction risks crushing bees between wooden parts due to clumsy handling. [Ullas Naik]
13. Traditional feeding requires opening the box, resulting in temperature loss and disturbance. [Dhruv Bhat, Sushanth Hegde]
14. Expensive foreign gear (like Flow Hives at ₹25,000+) is completely unviable for local farmers making 10kg/year. [Ullas Naik, Harsha Hegde]
15. A transparent window, while desired by beginners, will just be sealed with propolis by the bees because they need pitch darkness. [Harsha Hegde]
16. Unavailability of modular systems—new innovations often require discarding existing brood boxes, increasing costs. [Dhruv Bhat]

## Market & Economic Constraints
17. Beekeepers struggle to market their honey directly, being forced to sell at ₹400/kg to bulk buyers instead of ₹800/kg retail. [Harsha Hegde]
18. Total lack of technology and training to extract highly profitable by-products like Royal Jelly, Bee Venom, and Pollen. [Harsha Hegde]
19. Fear of micro-plastics entering the honey from modern plastic frames. [Dhruv Bhat]
