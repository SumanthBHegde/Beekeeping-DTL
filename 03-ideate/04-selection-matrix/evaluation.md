# Concept Selection Matrix

This document provides a rigorous, objective evaluation of the 6 final concepts identified during the dot-voting phase. The goal of this matrix is to filter the concepts down to the most viable solution(s) to carry forward into the `04-prototype` phase.

---

## 1. Selection Criteria

Each concept is scored on a scale of **1 to 5** (where 5 = Excellent/Perfect Fit, and 1 = Poor/Fails Constraint) across five specific criteria directly derived from our [Problem Statement](../../02-define/04-problem-statement.md) and [Scope Decision](../../02-define/06-scope-decision.md).

1. **Safety & Reduced Disturbance:** Does it actively reduce the risk of stings and prevent unnecessary hive opening?
2. **Cost & Retrofitting:** Does it fit the strict < ₹4,000 budget for small-scale farmers and attach to existing wooden boxes?
3. **Prototyping Feasibility:** Can the team realistically design, build, and test a physical prototype during this semester?
4. **Workload Impact:** Does it streamline daily maintenance or significantly reduce the frequency of physical labor?
5. **Core Problem Fit:** Does it directly answer the core problem: *"Safer and simpler inspection & maintenance"*?

## 3. Interpretation & Final Decision

### Score Breakdown
- **Top-Tier Concepts:**
  - Concept 1: Acrylic inspection window with sliding cover (**21/25**)
  - Concept 2: External sugar syrup feeder with outside refill (**21/25**)
- **Second-Tier Concepts:**
  - Concept 5: Donut ant/water ring (**18/25**)
  - Concept 3: Clip‑on modular super (**17/25**)
- **Third Tier:**
  - Concept 4: Auto-harvest system (**15/25** - High impact, but extremely low feasibility/high cost)
  - Concept 6: Internal insulation (**15/25** - Important, but indirect to the core inspection problem)

### Final Chosen Direction
Both of the top-scoring concepts directly support our final problem statement. They offer safer, simpler inspection and maintenance for small-scale farmers, serve as low-cost retrofits, and are highly realistic to prototype within the constraints of this course.

**Conclusion for Prototyping:**
Rather than building two separate products, our final chosen direction for prototyping is an **integrated “Safe Inspection & Feeding Upgrade” box.**

This ultimate prototype will combine:
1. The **Acrylic inspection window** with a sliding cover (handling the *Inspection* pain point).
2. The **External non‑intrusive feeder** with an outside refill funnel (handling the *Maintenance* pain point).

This combined solution elegantly and comprehensively answers our primary HMW questions.
