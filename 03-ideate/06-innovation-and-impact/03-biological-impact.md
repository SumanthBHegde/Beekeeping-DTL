# Biological Impact: Colony Health & Stability

While our solution was designed around the user's needs, the secondary benefits to the bee colony itself are arguably just as significant.

## The Importance of the Micro-Climate
A beehive is a highly regulated environment. Bees expend a massive amount of energy maintaining a specific internal temperature (around 35°C for the brood) and humidity level. Furthermore, they seal cracks with propolis to protect against pests and drafts.
- **The Traditional Problem:** Every time a beekeeper lifts the lid to check food levels or inspect for disease, that micro-climate is instantly destroyed. Heat escapes, and the propolis seal is broken. The bees must then spend days gathering resources and expending energy to re-establish that environment.
- **The Upgrade Impact:** By never lifting the lid for routine checks or feeding, the internal micro-climate is completely preserved. The bees save a tremendous amount of energy.

## Reduced Colony Stress
Opening a hive triggers an emergency alarm pheromone among the guard bees. This stress response disrupts the colony's normal foraging and nursing behaviors for hours. 
Our non-disruptive design means the bees can continue their natural workflows uninterrupted, even while the beekeeper is observing them through the window or pouring syrup into the feeder.

## Better Survival Rates
During harsh monsoons or dry summer months, starvation is a major threat. Often, farmers delay feeding the bees because opening the hive during bad weather is dangerous for both the farmer and the colony.
Because our external feeder allows feeding without opening the box, bees can be fed consistently and safely regardless of the external weather conditions, drastically improving the colony's chances of surviving the off-season.
