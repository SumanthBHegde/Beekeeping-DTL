# Economic Impact & ROI

The final piece of our impact analysis centers on the financial feasibility and return on investment (ROI) for the small-scale farmer.

## Ultra-Low Cost Implementation
Our design constraint dictated a budget of less than ₹4,000. Our solution costs significantly less:
- **Existing Asset:** Retains the ₹2,500 wooden box already owned by the farmer.
- **Upgrade Cost:** The cost of a small sheet of acrylic, a few PVC fittings, a funnel, and minor carpentry labor is estimated to be well under **₹500 per box**.

## The Return on Investment (ROI)
For an investment of less than ₹500, the financial returns are realized in several ways:

1. **Higher Honey Yields:** Because the bees are expending less energy re-regulating their micro-climate after inspections, and because they are not experiencing routine stress, that energy is redirected into brood rearing and foraging. Healthier colonies produce more surplus honey.
2. **Reduced Colony Loss:** By making feeding extremely easy, farmers are less likely to let their colonies starve during the monsoon. Saving just one colony from starvation saves the farmer the ₹1,500 - ₹2,000 cost of purchasing a new colony the following year.
3. **Labor Savings:** Time is money for a farmer. Reducing the time spent on hive maintenance allows the farmer to focus on their primary crops or scale up their apiary, managing more hives in the same amount of time.

## Conclusion
The **Safe Inspection & Feeding Upgrade** is not just a usability enhancement; it is a highly economical investment that directly protects the farmer's assets (the bees) while maximizing their potential output (the honey).
